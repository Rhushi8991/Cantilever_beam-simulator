Formulas & Theory Behind the Simulator

Beam Deflection

The deflection y (vertical displacement) of a cantilever beam at any point x from the fixed end, under a point load P applied at a distance a, is calculated as follows:

For x ≤ a:

y(x) = (P * x²) / (6 * EI) * (3a - x)

For x > a:

y(x) = (P * a²) / (6 * EI) * (3x - a)

Where E is the Young's Modulus and I is the Moment of Inertia.

Moment of Inertia (I)

The Moment of Inertia (I) is a property of a cross-section that measures its efficiency in resisting bending. It is also known as the second moment of area.

Circle: I = (π * r⁴) / 4
Rectangle: I = (b * h³) / 12
Triangle: I = (b * h³) / 36
